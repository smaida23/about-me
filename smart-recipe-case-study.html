Smart Recipe App – UI/UX Case Study

Designing a calm, AI-assisted cooking experience that helps users decide what to cook faster—and enjoy the process from discovery to the plate.

Role: UI/UX Designer Platform: iOS & Android Tools: Figma, Prototyping Type: Mobile App Concept

Project Overview

Smart Recipe is a mobile application concept built to simplify everyday meal decisions. It combines personalized recipe recommendations, smart ingredient suggestions, and lightweight meal planning in one cohesive experience. Users can quickly discover what to cook, save favorites, and follow clear step-by-step guidance based on their preferences, available time, and what they already have at home.

The Challenge

Recipe apps often fail not because users lack options—but because they offer too many, without structure. During research and competitive review, several recurring pain points emerged:

• Decision fatigue – Endless scrolling and comparison make it hard to choose a meal, especially on busy weekdays
• Weak filtering – Time, difficulty, and dietary needs are buried or inconsistent across screens
• Poor information hierarchy – Dense layouts make ingredients and instructions hard to scan in the kitchen
• Low repeat usage – Without favorites, planning, and save actions, users rarely return after the first session

The core problem was not missing content—it was friction in the journey from “What should I cook?” to “I’m cooking this now.”

Design Objectives

The goal was to design a calm, efficient cooking experience that reduces cognitive load and supports habit-building. Primary objectives included:

• Enable faster recipe discovery through personalization and smart filters
• Surface AI-assisted ingredient suggestions that respect what users already have
• Structure recipe detail screens for clarity: ingredients first, steps second, actions always visible
• Support weekly meal planning and saved favorites for repeat engagement
• Establish a warm, trustworthy visual system where food photography leads and UI stays out of the way

Research & Process

The UX process began with analyzing common patterns in cooking apps and mapping high-priority user journeys: onboarding preferences, home discovery, search and filter, recipe detail, save to favorites, and weekly planning.

Low-fidelity wireframes validated navigation depth and information hierarchy before visual design. Key decisions—such as image-first recipe cards, persistent filter chips, and a single primary action per recipe screen—were tested against the question: Can a user decide and start cooking in under a minute?

High-fidelity screens followed, focused on readability, reusable components, and a scalable design system suitable for future features (grocery lists, dietary profiles, seasonal collections).

Design Approach

The design philosophy centers on clarity, warmth, and scanability:

Visual strategy: A modern palette with purple-to-pink accents pairs with generous whitespace and large food imagery. Typography and contrast prioritize legibility when users glance at the screen while cooking.

Information architecture: Home emphasizes personalized picks and categories; filters live one tap away; recipe detail follows a predictable pattern so users never hunt for ingredients or steps.

Interaction design: Save, plan, and share actions use consistent placement and feedback. Progressive disclosure keeps advanced options available without overwhelming first-time users.

Key Features

Personalized Home Feed
Curated recipe suggestions based on preferences, past saves, and browsing behavior—reducing time spent searching.

Smart Ingredient Suggestions
AI-assisted recommendations that help users cook with what they already have, minimizing waste and last-minute store runs.

Quick Filters
Filter by preparation time, difficulty, and dietary needs so users can narrow options in seconds.

Recipe Detail & Cooking Mode
Clear ingredient lists, numbered step-by-step instructions, and visual hierarchy optimized for use in the kitchen.

Favorites & Meal Planning
Save recipes for quick access and organize meals across the week to support planning habits and return visits.

Solution

Smart Recipe delivers a structured end-to-end flow: discover → compare → decide → cook → save → plan again. Category-based browsing and smart filters shorten the path to a confident choice. Each recipe screen is built around scannable content and obvious actions, while personalization and ingredient intelligence address the “nothing to cook” moment that causes drop-off in competing apps.

The result is a product that feels approachable for everyday cooks and credible enough for users who cook often—without the clutter of a full social or content platform.

Outcome

The final concept presents a polished, user-friendly mobile experience that makes everyday cooking faster and more enjoyable. By combining personalization, clear interaction patterns, and a cohesive visual system, Smart Recipe is positioned to improve engagement, support repeat usage, and scale into adjacent lifestyle domains such as health, wellness, and grocery partnerships.

Key Takeaways

✓ Reduced decision friction through personalization, filters, and clear hierarchy
✓ Strengthened trust with image-led cards and consistent recipe detail layouts
✓ Designed for repeat use with favorites, meal planning, and save-first interactions
✓ Built a reusable component system ready for prototyping and future development handoff
